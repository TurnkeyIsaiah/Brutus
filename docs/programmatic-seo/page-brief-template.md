# Brutus Objection Page Brief Template

Use this brief before any sub-agent drafts a page.

## Mandatory writing skill, voice, and SEO playbook

Every draft, hand-written or sub-agent generated, must:

1. Invoke the `article-writing` skill (`~/.claude/skills/article-writing/SKILL.md`).
2. Mirror the voice reference at `voice-references/brutus-voice.md`.
3. Apply the Brutus SEO playbook at `../seo/playbook.md` (per-page requirements, similarity threshold, schema rules, do-not list).

House style sits on top: no em dashes mid-sentence, no emojis. See `subagent-workflow.md` for the full rule. Drafts that skip any of the three references, trip the skill's banned patterns, or break voice or playbook rules are rejected on first review.

## Required sections

The draft must include:

1. H1 that matches the query naturally
2. Unique intro, 120-180 words
3. 3-5 turn objection-specific dialog
4. Psychology section
5. Counter-frames sized to the actual objection, not a forced count
6. Common mistakes and fixes section
7. When to walk away section
8. Brutus-specific section
9. Related objections section
10. FAQ section with 2-3 unique questions

## Approval checklist

- The page is useful even without Brutus on it.
- The dialog could not be swapped onto 10 other pages.
- The psychology section is specific to this objection.
- The page does not force a fixed-count meaning structure if the objection needs a different shape.
- The correction section goes deeper than naming the mistake.
- The CTA is not copy-pasted from the last page.
- The FAQ is not recycled boilerplate.
