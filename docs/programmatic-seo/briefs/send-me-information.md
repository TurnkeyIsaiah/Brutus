# Brief: send-me-information

## Query and intent

- Target query: how to handle "send me information" sales objection
- Primary objection phrase: "send me some information"
- Search intent: informational with high commercial intent (reps who keep emailing decks into a void)
- Why this query deserves its own page: this is the most-rationalized loss in sales. Reps log it as progress because the prospect did not say no. They said no. Sending the deck and disappearing is the lowest-conversion follow-up motion in outbound. This page wins by naming that bluntly and giving reps a way to redirect inside the call.

## Differentiation

- Closest sibling pages: `not-interested`, `bad-timing`
- What makes this page meaningfully different from those siblings:
  - vs. `not-interested`: that is a 15-second reflex before the pitch. This is an end-of-call polite dismissal after the prospect has heard enough to want it to end.
  - vs. `bad-timing`: that is about priorities. This is about politeness and exit.
- What should not be repeated from other pages:
  - Do not include cold-call pattern-interrupt mechanics. Those are for `not-interested`.
  - Do not include "sense of urgency" framing. That is for `bad-timing`.
  - Do not include generic "ask what they want you to send" without the politeness-pivot framing.

## Required unique angle

- Required intro angle: "Send me some information" is the most-rationalized loss in sales. It feels like progress because they did not say no. They said no in a way that lets them end the call without being rude. Decks emailed after this objection convert near zero. The job inside the call is to convert the polite exit into a real next step or a clean disqualifier. Sending the deck and hoping is the worst of three options.
- Required psychology angle: politeness. The prospect wants to end the call without conflict. Saying "send me information" lets them end it cleanly. Most reps then take that exit because it lets them log a non-loss in the CRM.
- Required example scenario: discovery call where the rep started pitching too soon, the prospect's tone turns transactional, and the prospect asks for a one-pager. Show the rep agreeing too quickly and ending the call. Then show the corrected version where the rep asks one specific question that surfaces what would actually help the prospect decide.
- Required "when to walk away" condition: the prospect has a documented procurement process where info packets are a real evaluation step, you have confirmed you fit the criteria, and they have given you a specific decision date. Then sending materials is real work, not a brush-off.
- Required Brutus coaching moment: Brutus detects a "transactional pivot" in tone (shorter answers, more formal cadence) and prompts the rep to ask "what would you actually want me to send and why?" before agreeing to end the call. Specific Brutus prompt example: "Don't agree to send. Ask what would help them decide."

## Required sections

The draft must include:

1. H1 that matches the query naturally (e.g., "What to say when a prospect asks you to 'send me some information'")
2. Unique intro, 120-180 words, naming the most-rationalized-loss frame
3. 3-5 turn example dialog of a discovery call where the prospect's tone turns transactional and asks for a one-pager. Rep mishandles by agreeing fast. Rep corrects by asking the redirect question.
4. Psychology section anchored on politeness and the conflict-free exit
5. 3-5 counter-frames focused on redirecting the polite exit:
   - Redirect question: "What would you want me to include that would actually help you decide?"
   - Calendar pin: "Happy to send it. I will also drop a 5-minute placeholder for tomorrow. If it is helpful we talk. If not, decline the invite."
   - Specificity test: "When you say information, do you mean pricing, a case study from a peer, or a security one-pager?"
6. Common mistakes section: agreeing immediately, sending a generic deck, sending without a follow-up date, sending and going silent
7. When to walk away section
8. Brutus-specific section explaining how Brutus listens for transactional-pivot tone and prompts the rep to redirect before they end the call too cheaply
9. Related objections section linking to siblings
10. FAQ section with 2-3 unique questions (suggested: "Is sending the deck ever the right move?", "How fast should I follow up after sending?", "Is calendar-pin pushy?")
