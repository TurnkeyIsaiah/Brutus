# Brief: not-interested

## Query and intent

- Target query: how to handle "not interested" cold call objection
- Primary objection phrase: "not interested"
- Search intent: informational with high commercial intent (cold-call SDRs and outbound reps losing the first 15 seconds)
- Why this query deserves its own page: most ranking pages treat "not interested" as a real objection and try to overcome it with logic. That is wrong advice. The phrase is a reflex inside the first 15 seconds of an unexpected call, not a position the prospect can defend. This page wins by drawing that line and giving the right play for each side of it.

## Differentiation

- Closest sibling pages: `send-me-information`, `bad-timing`
- What makes this page meaningfully different from those siblings:
  - vs. `send-me-information`: that is a polite end-of-call dismissal where the prospect has heard the pitch. This is a reflex before the prospect has heard anything.
  - vs. `bad-timing`: that is about priorities and pipeline. This is about the cold-call adrenaline window.
- What should not be repeated from other pages:
  - Do not include "follow up later" advice. That is for `bad-timing`.
  - Do not include "ask what they would want you to send." That is for `send-me-information`.
  - Do not include generic empathy openers ("I get it, I called you out of the blue") without the pattern-interrupt mechanic attached.

## Required unique angle

- Required intro angle: "Not interested" said in the first 15 seconds of a cold call is a pattern, not a position. The prospect has not processed what you said. Treating it like a real objection is the fastest way to lose the call. Treating it like a pattern, and interrupting that pattern with permission, is what gets you another 30 seconds.
- Required psychology angle: cold-call adrenaline and autopilot dismissal. The prospect's brain registers "stranger on phone interrupting me" before it registers the words. The reflex fires before the meaning lands.
- Required example scenario: opening 10 seconds of an outbound call. Rep states the company name. Prospect says "not interested" while the rep is still in their second sentence. Show the rep mishandling it (defending, pitching harder), then show the corrected version (acknowledge, permission-based interrupt, one-line value frame).
- Required "when to walk away" condition: when you ask "what specifically isn't interesting?" and the prospect answers with a coherent disqualifier (wrong company size, no use case, hard policy against your category). That is a real not-interested. Move on.
- Required Brutus coaching moment: Brutus detects "blow-off" tone in the first 15 seconds (clipped response, voice not engaged) and surfaces a pattern-interrupt prompt before the rep starts pitching harder. Specific Brutus prompt example: "Don't sell harder. Ask for 30 seconds and a no."
