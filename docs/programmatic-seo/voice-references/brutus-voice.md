# Brutus Voice Reference

This is the source-of-truth voice spec for every objection page draft. The `article-writing` skill calls for voice references — these are ours, pulled directly from the live marketing site.

## Source pages sampled
- `website/index.html`
- `website/about.html`
- `website/features.html`
- `website/pricing.html`

## Voice in one line

Direct, lowercase, no-fluff sales coach with dry humor. Treats the reader like a rep who already knows they have problems and is tired of being lied to about them.

## Hard stylistic rules (verified on the live site)

1. **Mostly lowercase headings, with brand casing preserved.** "Brutus" always uses a capital B. "AI" stays uppercase. Do not force the brand name lowercase.
2. **Short paragraphs.** One to three sentences max. Often one fragment.
3. **Second person, present tense.** Address the rep directly. "you just interrupted them." not "reps often interrupt prospects."
4. **No em dashes mid-sentence.** Period, comma, or rephrase. (House rule, applies on top of skill.)
5. **No emojis.** Anywhere. (House rule.)
6. **Specific numbers when present.** "3-5 second feedback latency", "screen capture every 5 seconds", "40,000 tokens per session". Vague handwave numbers get cut.

## Signature moves (use these. they make a draft sound like Brutus.)

### Setup-then-payoff fragments
Pattern: state the broken thing, then drop the verdict in a fragment.

> "you sit through a call, make mistakes you don't even notice, lose the deal, and then days later someone tells you what you did wrong. by then it's too late. the prospect is gone. the damage is done."

### Negation framing
Pattern: define the brand by what it isn't. Use sparingly so it stays sharp.

> "no participation trophies."
> "no sugarcoating."
> "no subscriptions. no monthly fees."
> "Brutus doesn't care about your feelings. it cares about your results."

### Direct callout list
Pattern: short repeated structure with the same verb or stamp.

> "interrupting? called out. rambling? called out. feature-dumping? Brutus will not let it slide."

### Parenthetical aside for personality
Use occasionally. Once per page maximum. Keep it dry.

> "(also, yes, the stabbing thing. but mostly the hard truths part.)"

### Sting-then-promise close
End sections or pages with one line that admits the discomfort, then commits to the outcome.

> "yeah, it stings sometimes. but it works."
> "ready to get roasted? Brutus is waiting. go ahead and find out everything you're doing wrong."

### Concrete coaching-cue examples in body copy
When showing what Brutus does on a call, write the cue as Brutus would deliver it. Lowercase, short, second-person.

> "you just interrupted them. let them finish next time."
> "you've been talking for 90 seconds straight. ask a question."
> "feature dumping. focus on their problem, not your product."

## Phrases and patterns to never use

These break voice on contact:

- "In today's rapidly evolving sales landscape" or any landscape opener
- "Moreover", "Furthermore", "Additionally" as transitions
- "game-changer", "cutting-edge", "revolutionary", "transformative"
- "world-class", "best-in-class", "industry-leading"
- "leverage" used as a verb in marketing copy
- "synergy", "alignment", "ecosystem"
- Title case in headings
- Capital letters at the start of sentences (in body copy of pages that follow site convention)
- ROI promises with specific percentages we can't source
- "join thousands of reps" or any unverified scale claim
- AI-talk like "harness the power of" or "unlock your potential"

## Tone calibration notes

- **Confidence without ego.** Brutus the coach is tough but not cocky. The brand is on the rep's side, calling out their mistakes because no one else will.
- **Humor is dry, never goofy.** The Marcus Junius Brutus aside works because it lands once and moves on. Don't pile on jokes.
- **Honesty over hype.** When the brand acknowledges trade-offs (like "depends on the call. a quick 15-minute check-in uses way less than a 90-minute demo"), readers trust it more.
- **The reader is a rep, not a manager.** Write to the person on the call, not the person buying tools for a team.

> "Brutus is waiting. fair warning: it's not going to go easy on you."

## Application to objection pages

Every objection page draft should:

1. Open with a concrete example, dialog snippet, or sharp observation. Never with a definition or "in this article" framing.
2. Use lowercase headings throughout.
3. Keep body paragraphs to one or two short sentences.
4. Use at least one signature move (setup-then-payoff fragment, negation, callout list, sting-then-promise close, or parenthetical aside) per major section without overloading.
5. Write Brutus's coaching prompts in the same lowercase, fragment-y voice as the live overlay cues above.
6. Pass the article-writing skill's banned-pattern check and the house style rules.
